## Step 3: Confirmation and Planning

### Scheduling a Date

After you accept the quote, we schedule a specific date for treatment. When planning, we consider:

- **Meteorological conditions** – spraying requires suitable weather
- **The phenological stage** of the crop – for maximum effectiveness
- **Your schedule** – if you want to be present during treatment

We can typically schedule treatment within **3–7 days** of confirmation. In emergency situations (mass pest infestation), we respond considerably faster – sometimes within 24–48 hours.

### Route Preparation

Our team prepares the drone route in advance, using GPS coordinates and satellite imagery of your field. This includes:

- Determining exact field boundaries
- Identifying obstacles (poles, trees, power lines)
- Planning the direction of processing
- Identifying refilling zones

To better understand the technical side of the process, see our article on [how an agricultural drone works](/en/blog/how-agricultural-drone-works).

## Step 4: Preparing the Field (What You Need to Do)

### Before the Team Arrives

There are several simple things you can do to facilitate the process and ensure the best results:

**Ensure access to the field** – our team arrives with a vehicle loaded with the drone, batteries, water tank and preparations. Access to or near the field by light vehicle or pickup truck is required.

**Mark or remove temporary obstacles** – if you have irrigation hoses, temporary poles or other objects in the field, it is better to move them or mark them in advance.

**Provide water** (if possible) – clean water is needed for preparing the solution. If you have a well or water source near the field, this significantly speeds up the process. If not – our team comes prepared.

**Prepare the preparation** – if you are supplying the preparation, it should be available at the field when the team arrives. If we are supplying the preparation, it is included in the price.

**Inform your neighbours** – especially if they have apiaries nearby. Good neighbourly relations are important and legislation requires notification of beekeepers within a 3 km radius.

### What to Pay Attention To

- Temperature should be between 10°C and 25°C
- Wind should be light – up to 3–4 m/s
- No rain expected in the next 2–4 hours
- Dew should have dried (if spraying in the morning)

Our team monitors meteorological conditions and reschedules if needed to guarantee effectiveness.

## Step 5: Treatment Day

### Team Arrival

Our team arrives at the agreed location with all necessary equipment:

- **DJI Agras T50** – the main agricultural drone
- **Battery set** – ensuring continuous operation
- **Generator or charging unit** – for charging batteries in the field
- **Water tank and mixing unit** – for preparing the solution
- **Protective equipment** – for safe work with preparations

### Setup and Calibration

Before spraying begins, the operator:

1. Checks **meteorological conditions** on-site
2. Calibrates the **RTK system** for maximum navigation accuracy
3. Sets **spray parameters** – height, speed, rate, droplet size
4. Carries out a **test flight** to verify the system
5. Prepares the **working solution** according to the manufacturer's instructions

### The Spraying Itself

The drone takes off and follows the pre-set route. Here is what happens:

- The drone flies at a **height of 2–3 metres** above the crop
- It moves at a **speed of 5–8 m/s** depending on settings
- It covers a **strip of 8–11 metres width** at each pass
- It automatically **stops spraying** when turning and **resumes** when entering a new pass
- When the tank is empty, it **returns for refilling** and continues from the point where it stopped

You can observe the process from a safe distance. Many of our clients see an **agricultural drone spraying** for the first time and are impressed by the speed and precision.

### Duration

Processing time depends on the area:

- **50 decares** – approximately 20–30 minutes of actual spraying
- **200 decares** – approximately 1–1.5 hours
- **500 decares** – approximately 3–4 hours
- **1,000 decares** – a full day's treatment

These times include refilling. Total time in the field may be slightly longer due to setup and solution preparation.

## Step 6: After Treatment

### Report on Work Performed

After treatment is complete, you receive a detailed report that includes:

- **Map of the treated area** – exact outline of the sprayed zone
- **Total area** in decares
- **Amount used** of preparation and solution
- **Treatment time** – start and finish
- **Parameters** – height, speed, application rate
- **Meteorological conditions** during treatment

This report is useful for your documentation and can be used when proof of treatment performed is required (for example for subsidies or insurance purposes).

### What to Expect After Treatment

- **Herbicides** – the effect is visible after 5–14 days depending on the preparation
- **Fungicides** – the preventive effect is immediate, the curative effect – after 3–7 days
- **Insecticides** – the effect is rapid – usually within 24–48 hours
- **Foliar fertilisers** – improvement in colour and vitality is noticeable after 5–10 days

### Follow-Up Communication

Our team remains available for questions after treatment. If you notice anything unusual or have additional needs, simply contact us.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to be present during spraying?

It is not mandatory, but it is recommended, especially during the first treatment. That way you can see how the process works and ask questions.

### What happens in bad weather?

If meteorological conditions are not suitable, we reschedule for the next appropriate day. Our priority is the effectiveness of the treatment, not merely performing it.

### Can I enter the field after spraying?

Observe the pre-harvest interval specified by the preparation manufacturer. It is usually between 24 hours and 7 days depending on the product.

### What is the minimum area?

We work with areas from 20 decares upwards. For smaller areas, please contact us for an individual assessment.

### What if the drone breaks down during operation?

Our team carries backup equipment and can continue the work. We also have full insurance, so you carry no risk.
