## The Noon Mistake That Costs Dearly

I remember how last summer a farmer called us worried. He had hired a drone service, they treated his field during noon hours at 35 degrees, and a week later instead of healthy plants he saw burned leaves. "How so?" – he asked us. "I used the best product!"

The truth is that even the most expensive product cannot replace the right time for application.

## Why Does Timing Change Everything?

When the drone disperses the solution over the field, each droplet begins a journey. At high temperatures and low humidity, this journey ends before the droplet reaches the leaf – it simply evaporates in the air. In strong wind, droplets drift hundreds of meters sideways. In rain, they wash off before taking effect.

Weather isn't just a backdrop for work – it determines whether your investment will work or become an expensive lesson.

## Golden Time: Early Morning

Imagine the field at 6 in the morning. The air is calm, the temperature pleasant, dew forms a thin film on the leaves. When the drone passes at this moment, the solution falls on a wet surface and holds. No wind to carry away the droplets. No strong sun to evaporate them.

**Why it works:**

- Temperature is between 15-20°C – ideal for absorption
- Humidity is high (70-85%) – droplets don't evaporate
- Wind is calm – application precision
- Dew helps – better adhesion to leaves

However, there's a fine line: too much dew can dilute the solution. If your shoes get wet when stepping into the field – wait another half hour.

## Evening: The Second Great Opportunity

The sun sets, temperature drops, the air begins to carry moisture. Between 18:00 and 21:00 the second window for perfect spraying opens.

This time even has an advantage over morning – plants have the whole night to absorb the product without the stress of sunlight. For fungicides and insecticides, this is golden time.

**The only warning:** Check the rain forecast. If precipitation is expected in the next 3-4 hours, postpone. Otherwise, you'll watch your investment literally wash away.

## Noon: The Forbidden Zone

Between 11:00 and 17:00 hours the fields may look beautiful under bright rays, but for drone spraying this is the most dangerous zone.

At 30+ degrees and humidity below 50%, droplets begin to evaporate even before touching the plants. Product concentration increases instantly, which can cause phytotoxicity – leaf burn.

Add thermal vortices and unstable air to this – and you get the perfect recipe for losing money and damaging the crop.

## Seasonal Nuances

### Spring: Watch for Temperature Inversions

In spring, temperature can change dramatically between day and night. A May day can be 25 degrees, while the night drops to 5. This creates temperature inversions – cold air layers near the ground.

Under such conditions, spray between 9:00-11:00 when the ground has already warmed but it hasn't gotten too hot.

### Summer: Working in the Dark

In July and August, the best time is often between 5:00-7:00 in the morning or after 20:00 in the evening. Some farmers even work with lights between 21:00-23:00 to completely avoid the heat.

Modern drones with GPS and night vision make this completely possible and safe.

### Autumn: The Long Windows

Autumn is the most generous season for drone spraying. Temperatures are mild, humidity high, wind more predictable. The work window extends to 8:00-18:00.

But don't relax – always check weather conditions in real time, not just last night's forecast.

## The Practical Approach: What to Do Tomorrow?

If you need to treat the fields tomorrow, here's my advice:

1. **Check the forecast** for temperature, humidity, wind and rain for the whole day
2. **Plan for morning** (6:00-9:00) or evening (18:00-21:00)
3. **Go on site** an hour early and check actual conditions
4. **Measure** with a pocket weather instrument if you have one
5. **Be flexible** – if conditions aren't good, postpone

## The Price of Patience

I know a farmer who last year postponed treatment three times because conditions weren't suitable. His friends laughed that he was too worried. But when harvest time came, his field yielded 15% more than theirs.

He wasn't more worried. He simply understood that in agriculture, weather isn't a factor we can ignore – it's a partner we must work with.

## Conclusion

Drone spraying is incredible technology, but it cannot change the laws of physics. Droplets obey temperature, humidity, and wind. The best time for treatment isn't when it's convenient for you – it's when nature gives the green light.

Early morning or late evening. Mild temperatures and high humidity. Calm air and clear sky afterward. These are the ingredients of successful treatment.

And remember: it's better to wait for one perfect moment than to have to treat twice because the first one didn't work.
