## Corn Spraying with Drone – Crop Protection Without Harvest Losses

Corn is the second most important field crop in Bulgaria after wheat. With areas of over 500,000 hectares annually, it is of key importance for Bulgarian agriculture – both for grain production and for livestock fodder. But growing corn has one specific problem that every farmer knows: when the plants grow tall, ground machinery inevitably tramples and destroys part of the crop.

Corn drone spraying solves this problem completely. In this article we will look at why drone technology is the ideal solution for corn crop protection, when and how to spray, and what benefits it brings to farmers.

## The Main Problem: Corn Trampling with Ground Spraying

Corn is a tall crop – it reaches 2–3 metres at full growth. When treatment is needed in summer (herbicide, fungicide or insecticide), ground sprayers must pass through the crop. With every pass the machinery wheels destroy the plants in the row.

Statistics show that with conventional tractor spraying **3–5% of the crop is destroyed** by trampling. On a field of 1,000 decares this means loss of 30–50 decares of corn – a real financial loss that can be avoided.

### What This Means in Practice

At an average yield of 700–800 kg/dka and a price of 0.35–0.40 BGN/kg, the loss from trampling on 1,000 decares amounts to:

- 30–50 dka × 700 kg = 21,000–35,000 kg of lost grain
- Financial loss: 7,000–14,000 BGN

This loss is completely preventable through corn drone spraying. The drone flies above the crop and does not touch a single plant.

## When to Spray Corn with a Drone – Key Windows

### Early Herbicide Spraying (3–5 Leaf Phase)

In the early development phase corn is vulnerable to weed competition. [Herbicide spraying of corn](/en/blog/herbicide-spraying-corn) is most effective when plants are in the 3–5 leaf phase and weeds are still small.

With a drone this treatment is performed quickly and effectively, without risk of soil compaction, which is especially important at the start of vegetation.

### Late Herbicide Treatment

If a repeat herbicide treatment is needed, when corn is already taller (8–10 leaves), the drone is practically the only option for spraying without trampling. Ground machinery at this point would already cause significant damage.

### Fungicide Spraying

Corn is susceptible to a number of diseases:

- **Helminthosporium (leaf blight)** – one of the most common corn diseases in Bulgaria
- **Fusarium of the cob** – dangerous for both yield and grain quality
- **Rusts** – in wet and warm conditions

Fungicide treatment is usually applied around the tasselling phase. By this time corn is already tall and ground spraying is extremely difficult. The drone solves this problem – flying at 2–3 metres above the plants and providing uniform coverage.

### Insecticide Treatment

The main corn pests in Bulgaria include:

- **European corn borer** – causes significant damage, especially in southern regions
- **Wireworms** – damage the root system
- **Aphids** – vectors of viruses

In mass attack, rapid response is needed. The drone can treat the field within hours, before pests cause irreparable damage.

## Advantages of Corn Drone Spraying

### Zero Trampling Losses

This is undoubtedly the greatest advantage. With corn the difference between drone and ground spraying is most visible – 3–5% saved harvest is direct profit for the farmer.

### Speed of Treatment

The DJI Agras T50 treats up to 200 decares per hour. How many decares the drone can treat per day depends on organisation and conditions, but with optimal work [the drone can treat](/en/blog/how-many-hectares-drone-sprays-per-day) over 1,500 decares per day.

### Work in Wet Conditions

Summer in Bulgaria often brings thunderstorms. After rain, corn fields can be impassable for heavy machinery for days. The drone can work immediately after rain stops, without waiting for the soil to dry.

### Precise Dosing

The drone's GPS navigation guarantees that every part of the field receives the exact preparation dose. No gaps, no overlap. This is simultaneously more effective and more economical.

### Minimal Soil Impact

Heavy sprayers compact the soil, especially in wet conditions. Compaction hinders root system development and reduces yield. With drone spraying the soil remains untouched.

## Practical Tips for Farmers

### Plan in Advance

Do not wait for the problem to become critical. Contact AirAgro in advance to plan treatments. Determine when herbicide, fungicide and potentially insecticide spraying will be needed.

### Monitor Fields Regularly

Regular crop inspection helps with early identification of problems. When you notice the first signs of disease or pest attack, respond quickly – call AirAgro and we will organise spraying as quickly as possible.

### Consider Meteorological Conditions

Optimal conditions for corn drone spraying are:
- Temperature: 15–28°C
- Wind: up to 3–4 m/s
- Humidity: above 50%
- No rain in the next 2–3 hours

Spray early in the morning or in the evening, when temperatures are lower and evaporation is minimal.

### Combine Treatments

Where possible, combine fungicide and insecticide treatment in one pass. This saves time and costs. The AirAgro team will advise which preparations can be safely mixed.
