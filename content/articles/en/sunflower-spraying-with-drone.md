## Why Sunflower Needs Precise Protection

Sunflower is susceptible to a number of diseases and pests that can reduce yield by 20–50% in unfavourable conditions. The main threats are:

### Sunflower Diseases

- **White rot (Sclerotinia sclerotiorum)** – one of the most dangerous diseases, can destroy entire fields. Develops in wet conditions and can cause losses up to 50% of the harvest
- **Downy mildew** – causes systemic disease of plants, leaf and capitulum deformation
- **Phomopsis (Diaporthe helianthi)** – affects stalks and causes their bending, especially at ripening
- **Alternaria** – causes dark spots on leaves and capitula, worsens seed quality
- **Grey rot (Botrytis)** – attacks capitula in wet weather

### Pests

- **Sunflower moth (Homoeosoma nebulella)** – larvae feed on seeds in the capitulum
- **Aphids** – vectors of viral diseases
- **Wireworms** – damage the root system

Timely sunflower drone spraying enables fast and effective intervention against any of these threats.

## When to Spray Sunflower – Optimal Moments

To understand in detail [when to spray sunflower](/en/blog/when-to-spray-sunflower), it is important to know the crop's development phases.

### Herbicide Treatment (2–6 Leaf Phase)

Weed control is of primary importance in the early development phases of sunflower. Weeds compete for water, light and nutrients and can significantly reduce yield.

With a drone, herbicide treatment is performed fast and without soil compaction. Precise GPS navigation guarantees uniform coverage without gaps.

### Fungicide Treatment (Budding Phase)

In the budding phase (BBCH 51–55) is the appropriate moment for prophylactic fungicide treatment against Sclerotinia and Phomopsis. The drone provides excellent coverage of the leaf mass and the upper part of the plants.

### Treatment During Flowering

If diseases are identified during flowering, drone spraying is the preferred method. Sunflower is already tall (1.5–2 metres) and ground machinery would cause significant trampling. The DJI Agras T50 flies above the crop and treats effectively without damaging a single plant.

### Late Treatment (Seed Filling)

In this phase additional fungicide spraying may be needed, especially in wet conditions when the risk of grey rot and Alternaria is elevated. The drone is practically the only effective way to treat in this phase.

## Advantages of Drone Spraying for Sunflower

### Zero Crop Trampling

As with corn, sunflower reaches significant height. Ground sprayers trample 3–5% of the crop with every pass. With a drone this loss is zero.

### Precision Coverage

The DJI Agras T50 that AirAgro uses disperses preparations with fine dispersion, providing excellent coverage of the leaf surface. The special nozzles and the airflow from the drone rotors help the preparation reach even the underside of leaves.

### Speed and Effectiveness

The drone treats up to 200 decares per hour, meaning a large field of 1,000–2,000 decares can be treated in a single day. This is critical when disease threatens, where every lost day means greater damage.

### Work in Complex Conditions

After summer rains fields can be inaccessible to heavy machinery. The drone can work immediately, without waiting for the soil to dry. A detailed comparison between the two methods can be found in the article [drone or tractor for spraying](/en/blog/drone-vs-tractor-spraying).

### Reduced Water and Preparation Use

The drone works with Ultra Low Volume (1–2 litres per decare), meaning significantly less water consumption and more efficient use of preparations.

## Practical Recommendations for Sunflower Growers

### Prevention Is the Key

Do not wait for disease to develop massively. Prophylactic fungicide spraying in the budding phase is much more effective than attempts to treat an already developed infection.

### Choose the Right Moment

Spray in early morning hours or in the evening when:

- Temperature is between 15°C and 28°C
- Wind is up to 3–4 m/s
- Humidity is above 50%
- No rain expected in the next 2–3 hours

### Preparation Rotation

To prevent resistance development, change active substances from season to season. The AirAgro team can advise on appropriate preparations.

### Monitor Fields

Regular inspection of sunflower fields allows early identification of problems. At first signs of disease, contact AirAgro for rapid response.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can the drone cover large sunflower areas?

Yes. With the DJI Agras T50 it is possible to treat 1,500–2,000 decares per day. For very large areas AirAgro can organise work with multiple drones simultaneously.

### Is the drone effective against Sclerotinia?

The drone is a means of applying the preparation, and effectiveness depends on the choice of fungicide and timeliness of treatment. With the right moment and preparation selected, drone spraying is extremely effective against Sclerotinia.

### Can preparations be combined in one spraying?

Yes, provided the preparations are compatible. Combining a fungicide with an insecticide or foliar fertilizer is commonly practised. The AirAgro team will advise on appropriate combinations.
