## Benefits of Drones in Agriculture – 10 Reasons to Choose a Drone

Agricultural drones are no longer an exotic novelty – they are a reality on fields in Bulgaria and worldwide. More and more farmers are discovering the **benefits of drones in agriculture** and finding that this technology solves problems they have been struggling with for years. From precision spraying to access to difficult terrain – drones offer dozens of advantages over traditional methods.

In this article we examine **10 key benefits** that make the agricultural drone an indispensable tool for the modern farmer. Each is supported by real data and practical examples from our work with the **DJI Agras T50**.

## 10 Key Benefits of Drones in Agriculture

### 1. Precision Spraying – the Right Dose in the Right Place

One of the most important **benefits of drones in agriculture** is precision. The DJI Agras T50 is equipped with centrifugal nozzles that create droplets of controlled size – neither too coarse (to run off) nor too fine (to be carried away by the wind). The flow control system regulates output in real time relative to flight speed, so that **every square metre receives the same dose**.

The airflow from the propellers (the so-called downwash effect) presses the droplets downward and distributes them evenly across the leaf mass. This means better coverage and higher effectiveness of preparations. The difference from conventional spraying is visible – especially in dense stands, where the tractor sprayer has difficulty achieving even coverage.

### 2. No Crop Trampling – 100% of the Area Preserved

With every pass of a tractor with a sprayer, **3–5% of the area** is directly trampled or receives mechanical damage. With 1,000 decares this means 30–50 dka of lost harvest – every year, with every treatment.

The drone flies at 2–3 metres above the crop and **does not touch a single plant**. Zero trampling means more harvest and healthier plants. With expensive crops such as rapeseed or vegetables, this difference can amount to hundreds of lev per hectare. A detailed comparison can be found in our article on [drone or tractor for spraying](/en/blog/drone-vs-tractor-spraying).

### 3. Speed – More Decares in Less Time

The DJI Agras T50 can treat up to **210 decares per hour** under optimal conditions. In practice daily productivity varies between **1,500 and 3,000 decares** – depending on parcel shape, dosage and logistics.

For comparison, a tractor with a sprayer treats 200–400 decares per day. The drone is **5 to 10 times faster**, which is critically important for urgent treatments – for example when a rain forecast means the field must be treated today. Read more about [how many decares a drone sprays per day](/en/blog/how-many-hectares-drone-sprays-per-day).

### 4. Water Savings – 5 to 10 Times Less

Tractor sprayers work with 15–30 litres of water per decare. The drone uses only **1–2 litres per decare** – with concentrated solutions and precision nozzles that provide the optimal droplet size.

What does this mean in practice? For treatment of 1,000 decares:

- **Tractor:** 15,000–30,000 litres of water, water tanker, pump, logistics
- **Drone:** 1,000–2,000 litres of water, ordinary cans

In conditions of drying out, characteristic of many areas in Southern Bulgaria – including around **Plovdiv, Stara Zagora, Pazardzhik and Haskovo** – the minimal water consumption of the drone is a huge advantage. Farms in the **Thracian Plain** and **Dobrudzha** especially value this benefit. No water source needed near the field, no heavy logistics.

### 5. Chemical Savings – up to 30% Saving

Precision spraying means **less waste** of expensive fungicides, insecticides and herbicides. Uniform coverage and controlled droplet size guarantee that the preparation lands where it is needed – on the plant, not on the soil.

Practice shows **up to 30% savings** on preparations when using a drone compared to conventional tractor spraying. At annual chemical costs of 50,000 BGN, this means **15,000 BGN** saved – without compromising effectiveness. For current [drone spraying prices](/en/blog/drone-spraying-price) and savings calculations, contact us.

### 6. Access to Difficult Terrain – No Impossible Field

Hilly parcels, steep slopes, waterlogged areas, small irregularly shaped parcels, vineyards, orchards – for a tractor all of this is a problem. For a drone – not. It **flies above any terrain** without depending on soil condition or slope.

In Bulgaria hilly and semi-hilly areas constitute a significant share of agricultural land. Many farmers in the Rhodopes, Balkan Range and Middle Balkan, as well as those with hilly parcels near **Pazardzhik** and **Haskovo**, have no alternative for spraying – the tractor simply cannot enter. The drone is the **only mechanised solution** for these terrains.

### 7. Night Work – When Conditions Are Best

A little-known fact: **night hours are ideal for spraying**. Wind is weaker, temperature is lower, humidity is higher – three factors that improve preparation effectiveness and reduce droplet drift.

The DJI Agras T50 is equipped with LED lighting, obstacle avoidance radar and full autonomous GPS navigation. It **works equally effectively day and night** – unlike the tractor, which is limited by visibility and safety. Night drone spraying is yet another of the key benefits of drones in agriculture, difficult to achieve with other machinery.

### 8. GPS Accuracy – to 1–2 cm with RTK System

Modern agricultural drones use **RTK (Real-Time Kinematic) GPS**, which provides accuracy to 1–2 centimetres. What does this mean for the farmer?

- **No overlaps** – the drone does not spray the same spot twice
- **No gaps** – every strip is treated
- **Precise tracking** of parcel edges – no preparation waste outside the field
- **Repeatability** – the route is saved and can be reused

GPS precision is the foundation of precision agriculture and is significantly better than the visual orientation of a tractor driver. Learn more about the technology in our article on [how agricultural drones work](/en/blog/how-agricultural-drones-work).

### 9. Environmental Benefits – Cleaner Agriculture

The environmental benefits of drones are multi-layered:

- **Less chemicals** in the environment thanks to precision dosing
- **Reduced drift** – less contamination of neighbouring areas and water sources
- **No soil compaction** – tractor ruts disrupt soil structure
- **Less fuel** – the drone runs on electricity, does not burn diesel
- **Less water** – 5–10 times lower water consumption

In the era of sustainable agriculture, reducing the chemical footprint is not only responsible behaviour but also a **competitive advantage** – especially for farms operating under European programmes or quality standards.

### 10. Labour Savings – One Operator, Thousands of Decares

Traditional tractor spraying requires a qualified tractor driver, an assistant for refilling, a water tanker driver. With drone spraying the operation is performed by **one trained operator** who monitors the autonomous flight from the ground.
