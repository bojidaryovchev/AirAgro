## Agricultural Regions of Bulgaria

### Thracian Plain – the Heart of Bulgarian Agriculture

The Thracian Plain is the largest and most productive agricultural region in Bulgaria. Stretching from Pazardzhik in the west to Svilengrad in the east, it encompasses the Plovdiv, Stara Zagora and Haskovo regions.

**Main crops:** wheat, corn, sunflower, tobacco, cotton, vegetables, vineyards, orchards

**Characteristics:** fertile alluvial soil, mild climate, farms varying in size from small vegetable gardens to large grain tracts.

AirAgro serves Thrace with teams based in the region. We cover:

- [Drone spraying in Plovdiv](/en/blog/drone-spraying-plovdiv) and the Plovdiv area
- [Drone spraying in Stara Zagora](/en/blog/drone-spraying-stara-zagora) and the region
- [Drone spraying in Haskovo](/en/blog/drone-spraying-haskovo) and the Haskovo area

Thrace requires intensive crop protection due to the warm climate, which favours the development of both crops and their pests. The DJI Agras T50 is the ideal tool in this fight – fast, precise and effective.

### Dobrudzha – the Breadbasket of Bulgaria

Northeastern Bulgaria, known as Dobrudzha, is the largest grain-producing region in the country. With its black-earth soils and flat terrain, Dobrudzha produces over 40% of Bulgaria's grain.

**Main crops:** wheat, corn, sunflower, rapeseed, barley

**Characteristics:** large consolidated plots (500–5,000+ decares), black-earth soil, intensive mechanised agriculture.

[Drone spraying in Dobrudzha](/en/blog/drone-spraying-dobrudja) complements existing ground machinery, especially for urgent treatments, wet terrain and difficult-to-access areas. For large farms we offer special prices and seasonal subscriptions.

### Danubian Plain – Extensive Arable Land

The Danubian Plain encompasses the regions of Pleven, Vratsa, Montana, Lovech and Vidin. Here grain and industrial crops are grown on large areas, and agriculture is the main livelihood of the local population.

**Main crops:** wheat, corn, sunflower, sugar beet, tobacco (in the western parts)

**Characteristics:** flat to hilly terrain, forest-steppe soils, medium to large farms.

Drone spraying on the Danubian Plain is especially effective for:

- Spring herbicide treatments of grain crops
- Fungicide sprayings of wheat and barley
- Insecticide treatments of corn and sunflower
- Foliar fertilization at critical development phases

### Rose Valley – Unique Microclimate

The Kazanlak Valley, known as the Rose Valley, is a unique agricultural area between the Balkan Range and the Sredna Gora. Here the oil-bearing rose and lavender are cultivated – crops of exceptionally high value.

**Main crops:** oil-bearing rose, lavender, grain crops, orchards

**Characteristics:** basin terrain sheltered from winds, specific microclimate, delicate high-value crops.

The DJI Agras T50 is ideal for spraying rose and lavender plantations – the fine mist does not damage the delicate flowers, and the precise application guarantees effective protection.

### Southwestern Bulgaria – Fruit and Vegetables

The Blagoevgrad and Kyustendil regions are known for fruit growing and vegetable production. The Kyustendil apples and cherries from the Blagoevgrad area are traditional Bulgarian products with high market value.

**Main crops:** apples, cherries, plums, grapes, vegetables, tobacco

**Characteristics:** basin and semi-mountain terrain, small to medium farms, intensive orcharding.

Drone spraying is especially valuable in Southwestern Bulgaria due to the hilly terrain and small plots that make the use of large ground machinery difficult.

### Pazardzhik Region – Vegetable Capital

Drone spraying in Pazardzhik is among the most sought-after services, as the region is the centre of vegetable production in Bulgaria. Tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, strawberries and peaches are grown on dozens of farms.

### Black Sea Region

The coastal regions of Burgas and Varna combine grain production with viticulture and vegetable growing. The specific maritime climate creates unique conditions for fungal diseases, making timely protection critically important.

As of 2026, drone spraying in Bulgaria is regulated by the General Directorate of Civil Aviation and the Bulgarian Food Safety Agency (**BFSA**). AirAgro operates with a full set of licences and certificates: **BFSA** licence for plant protection activities, **RHI** permit for work with plant protection products, certificates from **DJI Academy** for professional work with agro drones and status as **Professional User of PPP** (Plant Protection Products). All treatments are documented with GPS records and comply with the requirements of the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) of the EU.
