## Spraying with an Agri Drone

### What It Involves

Spraying is the main and most sought-after service for agricultural drones. The process involves aerial application of liquid preparations – herbicides, fungicides, insecticides and foliar fertilisers – directly onto the crops.

The DJI Agras T50 has a 40-litre tank and 8 nozzles that provide uniform coverage with precisely controlled droplet size. Thanks to the powerful rotors, the airflow created lifts the leaves and allows the preparation to reach the underside as well – something that ground sprayers rarely achieve.

### Types of Treatment

All standard types of spraying can be carried out with an agricultural drone:

**Herbicide treatment** – weed control in the early development stages of the crop. The drone can operate on wet soil when a tractor cannot enter the field.

**Fungicide treatment** – prevention and treatment of fungal diseases. Particularly effective for wheat (against powdery mildew, rust, septoria), barley and rapeseed.

**Insecticide treatment** – rapid response to pest infestations. The speed of the drone is of decisive importance here – the faster treatment is applied, the smaller the damage.

**Desiccation** – drying the crop before harvest for uniform ripening.

For more detailed information on crop protection, read our article on [drone for crop protection](/en/blog/drone-for-crop-protection).

### Who Is It Suitable For

Drone spraying is suitable for all types of farms – from small family operations to large agricultural holdings. It is especially useful when:

- Wet conditions prevent ground machinery from entering
- Tall maize or other tall crops
- Hilly terrain
- Emergency treatments where timing is critical
- Areas without convenient access for heavy machinery

## Fertilising with an Agri Drone

### What It Involves

In addition to liquid preparations, the DJI Agras T50 can also spread granular fertilisers via a specialised spreading module. This module is mounted in place of the liquid tank and allows precise aerial application of granular fertilisers directly from above.

### Advantages Over Traditional Fertilising

**Uniform distribution** – the drone follows a precise GPS route and spreads fertiliser at a constant rate, without gaps or overlaps. This is a significant improvement compared to manual or semi-mechanised fertilising.

**No soil compaction** – the tractor-mounted spreader compacts the soil with every pass. The drone flies at 3–5 metres height and does not affect the physical properties of the soil.

**Feeding of a growing crop** – when the crop is already tall and the tractor cannot enter without damage, the drone is the only effective solution for applying granular fertiliser.

**Speed** – the drone can cover 150–200 decares per hour when fertilising, which is considerably faster than ground methods.

### Types of Fertilisers Suitable for Drone Application

- Ammonium nitrate
- Urea (carbamide)
- Compound NPK fertilisers in granular form
- Potassium and phosphorus fertilisers
- Specialised microelement granules

### Practical Scenarios for Bulgarian Farmers

In Bulgarian agriculture, drone fertilising is especially useful in the following situations:

- **Early spring topdressing of wheat** – when fields are still wet from winter precipitation and tractors get stuck
- **Maize topdressing at 6–8 leaf stage** – when the crop is already too tall for ground machinery
- **Fertilising on sloping terrain** – in foothill and mountain areas, where tractors cannot safely operate
- **Rapid feeding under stress conditions** – drought, hail or other adverse conditions

## Crop Mapping and Monitoring

### What It Involves

Drone mapping is a service that is gaining increasing importance in precision agriculture. Through specialised cameras and sensors, the drone photographs the field and creates detailed maps showing the condition of the crops.

### Types of Mapping

**Orthophoto maps** – high-resolution images of the field from which an accurate map is created. They allow you to see every section of the field in detail.

**NDVI maps** – vegetation index maps showing the health of the plants. Green zones mean healthy plants, yellow and red ones – problem areas.

**Differential application maps** – based on NDVI data, maps are created showing where more fertiliser or preparation is needed and where less. This is the foundation of precision agriculture.

### Benefits of Mapping

**Early detection of problems** – diseases, weeds, nutrient deficiency – all these problems are visible on the maps before they become visible to the naked eye.

**Resource optimisation** – instead of treating the entire field with a uniform dose, you can apply more preparation or fertiliser only where needed.

**Documentation** – maps serve as documentation of the field condition at different points in the season. Useful for planning and for participation in subsidy programmes.

**Damage assessment** – after hail, flooding or other disasters, drone mapping provides an objective assessment of the affected area.
