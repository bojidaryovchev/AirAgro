## Drone Spraying Price – What You Need to Know in 2026

One of the first questions every farmer asks when they hear about **drone spraying** is: "How much does it cost?" And that is entirely understandable – farming is a business where every unit of currency per decare matters. In this article we will examine in detail what determines **the price of drone spraying**, how it compares with traditional methods and why in many cases drone services are more cost-effective than they appear at first glance.

## Factors That Determine the Price of Drone Spraying

**The price of drone spraying** is not fixed – it depends on numerous factors, each of which affects the final value. Let us look at the most important ones.

### Plot Size

This is the primary factor determining the price per decare. The logic is simple – the larger the area, the lower the price per decare. With larger farms, economies of scale are achieved – the costs of mobilisation, transport and setup are distributed across more decares.

For small areas of 20–50 decares the price per decare is higher, as the fixed costs are spread across a smaller area. For areas of 200–500 decares and above the price per decare decreases significantly. For farms over 1,000 decares, special terms are usually negotiated.

### Crop Type

Different crops require different settings and approaches. For example:

- **Cereal crops** (wheat, barley, rapeseed) are treated relatively easily – flat terrain, standard flight height. Typical treatments include **herbicide spraying**, **fungicide treatment** and **foliar feeding**
- **Tall maize** requires more careful parameter adjustment, especially for **insecticide** treatment against the European corn borer
- **Vineyards and orchards** need a specialised spraying mode with smaller droplets and lower speed for effective **crop protection**
- **Vegetable crops** may have specific requirements for application rate with **fungicides**, **herbicides** and **insecticides**

### Terrain and Accessibility

Flat-terrain plots are easier and faster to process. Hilly terrain, the presence of obstacles (poles, trees, power lines), as well as distance from the road network can increase the cost of the service.

### Number of Treatments

Many farmers plan several treatments within a season – herbicide, fungicide and foliar. When negotiating a package of several treatments, **the price of drone spraying** per decare is usually more favourable than for a single application.

### Preparation Type and Application Rate

Some preparations require a larger volume of solution per decare, meaning more refills and longer processing time. The standard rate for drone spraying is between 1 and 3 litres per decare, but for certain preparations it can reach 5 litres per decare.

## Indicative Price Ranges

Specific prices vary depending on the provider, region and conditions. However, we can outline indicative ranges for the Bulgarian market in 2026:

### Small Areas (up to 100 decares)

For smaller areas the price per decare is higher due to fixed mobilisation costs. However, farmers with such areas rarely own their own ground sprayer, making drone services a very practical alternative.

### Medium Areas (100–500 decares)

This is the optimal range where **drone spraying** shows the best price-to-quality ratio. Fixed costs are distributed evenly and the volume is sufficient for efficient drone operation.

### Large Areas (over 500 decares)

For large farms the price per decare is lowest. Here drone services compete directly with ground sprayers and in many cases offer a more cost-effective solution when all factors are taken into account.

## Comparison: Drone vs. Tractor – Which Is More Cost-Effective?

When comparing **the price of drone spraying** with the price of ground spraying, it is important to include all costs, not just the direct price per decare.

### Direct Costs of Ground Spraying

If you own your own sprayer, you need to include:

- Fuel for the tractor
- Equipment depreciation
- Maintenance and repairs
- Operator's wages
- Water for preparing the solution (considerably larger volume)

### Hidden Costs of Ground Spraying

These are the costs that many farmers overlook:

- **Harvest loss** from trampled plants in the tramlines – typically 3–5% of the area
- **Soil compaction** – leads to reduced yield in following seasons
- **Higher preparation consumption** – ground sprayers often over-apply by 15–30%
- **Time loss** – ground machinery is 3–5 times slower

### The Real Picture

When you add up all these factors, **drone spraying** often proves to be more cost-effective, especially when:

- Wet conditions prevent the tractor from entering the field
- Tall maize or other crops where ground machinery causes damage
- Emergency treatments where delay means yield loss
- Difficult-to-access terrain
