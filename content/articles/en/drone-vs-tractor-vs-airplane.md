## Introduction

In modern agriculture, there are three main methods for applying plant protection products (PPP): ground (tractor), aerial (airplane/helicopter), and drones. Let's examine each in detail.

## 1. Tractor (Ground Application)

<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Advantages ✅</th>
<th>Disadvantages ❌</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>High precision for row crops</td>
<td>Significant crop damage (up to 8-10% of area)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Good penetration into plant mass</td>
<td>Soil compaction (critical in wet conditions)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Low ownership cost (if you already have a tractor)</td>
<td>Inability to work with tall crops</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Familiar method for most farmers</td>
<td>Slow work (2-3 ha/hour average)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Suitable for small plots</td>
<td>High fuel and maintenance costs</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

### Average Costs

- **Investment:** 50,000-150,000 BGN (tractor + sprayer)
- **Service price:** 15-25 BGN/decare
- **Operating costs:** High (fuel, maintenance)

## When Is Each Method Most Suitable?

### Choose TRACTOR when:

- You have small plots (under 20 decares)
- Working with low row crops
- Soil is dry and firm
- You have your own equipment

### Choose AIRPLANE when:

- Processing over 500 decares at once
- You have an emergency situation (mass infestation)
- Difficult terrain access
- You have a large budget

### Choose DRONE when:

- Area: 20-500 decares (optimal)
- You want maximum precision
- Working with tall crops (corn, sunflower)
- Soil is wet/muddy
- Ecology matters to you
- You want documented proof of treatment

## Future Trends

📈 **Drones are the future** of precision agriculture:

- Integration with AI and machine learning

- Real-time monitoring and correction
- Integration with Weather API for optimal timing
- Multi-spectral cameras for early diagnosis

## Conclusion

Every method has its place in modern agriculture. For most Bulgarian farmers with areas of 50-500 decares, **drones offer the best ratio between price, quality and efficiency**.

The combination of high precision, zero crop damage, and competitive pricing makes agricultural drones the preferred choice for modern crop production.
