## How Drone Spraying Works

The process of spraying with an agri drone is considerably simpler than most people imagine. Here is what a standard treatment includes:

### Pre-Treatment Preparation

Before taking off, our team surveys the terrain. We use GPS coordinates and satellite imagery to plan the exact drone route. This includes determining field boundaries, identifying obstacles such as poles or trees, and the direction of processing.

### Parameter Configuration

Each crop and each preparation requires different settings. We adjust flight height, travel speed, droplet size and the spray rate. The DJI Agras T50 has an intelligent nozzle control system that guarantees uniform coverage of every square metre.

### The Spraying Itself

The drone follows the pre-set route with centimetre precision. Thanks to RTK navigation, overlap between adjacent passes is minimal and missed sections are practically non-existent. One DJI Agras T50 can process up to 200 decares per hour – a speed unachievable by most ground sprayers.

### Monitoring and Reporting

After treatment is complete, you receive a full report – a map of the treated area, execution time and amount of preparation used. This transparency is important to you as the farm manager, because you know exactly what has been done on your field.

## Advantages of Drone Spraying Over Traditional Methods

### Speed and Efficiency

One of the most tangible advantages of **drone spraying** is speed. While a tractor with a mounted sprayer processes an average of 30–50 decares per hour, the DJI Agras T50 covers up to 200 decares in the same time. This means that even large areas can be treated within a day.

### No Soil Compaction

Tractors and self-propelled sprayers inevitably compact the soil when passing through. This is particularly problematic in wet conditions or with delicate crops in early stages. The drone flies at a height of 2–3 metres above the crops and **does not touch the soil**. No tramlines, no yield loss from trampled plants.

### Precise Dosing

Traditional sprayers often apply more preparation than necessary, especially at turns and at pass overlaps. The agri drone works with a precise flow control system that automatically stops spraying when turning and resumes when entering a new pass. The result is **up to 30% less preparation consumption** at the same or better effectiveness.

If you are undecided between a drone and a tractor, read our detailed [comparison of drone and tractor for spraying](/en/blog/drone-vs-tractor-spraying).

### Access to Difficult-to-Reach Terrain

In Bulgaria, a considerable proportion of arable land lies on hilly terrain, with sloping sections, or next to water bodies. A tractor cannot work normally on a slope of more than 8–10 degrees. The drone, however, is not affected by terrain slope – it flies at a uniform height above the crop, regardless of the relief.

### Speed in Emergency Treatments

When there is a mass pest or disease outbreak, time is of the essence. Mobilising aviation takes days. A tractor may not be available exactly when needed. **Drone spraying** can be organised within hours, giving farmers an invaluable advantage at critical moments.

## When Is It Best to Spray with a Drone

The timing of treatment is a key factor for the effectiveness of any spraying. There are several main rules:

- **Early morning or evening** – when the wind is light and the temperature is lower, reducing evaporation of the preparation
- **Wind speed up to 3–4 m/s** – the DJI Agras T50 can operate in moderate wind but for optimal results we aim for calm conditions
- **No rain in the next 2–4 hours** – to allow the preparation to be absorbed
- **When the crops are at the appropriate phenological stage** – our specialists help determine the optimal moment

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many decares can a drone process in one day?

The DJI Agras T50 processes up to **200 decares per hour** under standard conditions. In one working day we can cover between 800 and 1,500 decares depending on the crop, terrain and number of refills. This is 3–5 times faster than a ground sprayer.

### Is drone spraying safe for bees?

Yes, drone spraying is **considerably safer** for bees compared to ground methods. Precise application reduces the drift of preparations outside the treated zone. Nevertheless, legislation requires notification of beekeepers within a radius of 3 km, which we strictly comply with.

### Can you spray with a drone during rain or strong winds?

We do not recommend spraying in winds above 4 m/s or if rain is expected in the next 2–4 hours. Our team monitors meteorological conditions and reschedules treatment if needed to guarantee maximum preparation effectiveness.

### What is the minimum area for drone spraying?
